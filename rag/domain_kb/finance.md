# Finance Glossary

## EBITDA
Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A measure of operating profitability that excludes the effects of capital structure and accounting choices.
- EBITDA margin = EBITDA / Revenue
- Healthy margins are industry-dependent: software 25-40%, retail 5-15%, manufacturing 10-20%.
Negative EBITDA in mature businesses typically signals operational distress; in early-stage growth companies it may reflect deliberate reinvestment.

## P/E Ratio (Price to Earnings)
Stock price divided by earnings per share. Reflects how much investors pay per dollar of earnings.
- S&P 500 historical average: roughly 15 to 20.
- Above 30: typically growth-stock territory.
- Negative or undefined: company has no earnings, ratio is not meaningful.

## DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)
Average number of days to collect payment after a sale. Measures receivables efficiency.
- DSO = (Accounts Receivable / Total Credit Sales) * Number of Days
- Industry benchmarks: roughly 30-45 days for B2B; > 60 days suggests collection issues or extended customer terms.

## Current Ratio
Current Assets / Current Liabilities. Measures short-term liquidity.
- < 1.0: potential liquidity stress.
- 1.5 to 3.0: typically healthy.
- > 3.0: may indicate inefficient asset deployment.

## Revenue Growth
Year-over-year change in revenue.
- Mature industries: 3-7% considered healthy.
- Growth companies: 20%+ expected.
- Negative growth in non-cyclical sectors warrants investigation.
